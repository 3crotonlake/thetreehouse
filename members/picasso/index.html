Pablo Picasso was born in Málaga, Spain in 1881. He co-founded Cubism, invented collage as a fine art medium, and produced an estimated 20,000 works across painting, drawing, sculpture, ceramics, and printmaking.

The 347 Series was made between March and October 1968, when Picasso was 86 years old. Working with master printers Aldo and Piero Crommelynck, he produced 347 etchings in seven months — teeming with musketeers, circus performers, painters and their models. Picasso described it as "my way of writing fiction."
