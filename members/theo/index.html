Artist statement

Painting what
was there.

Displacement is my subject — not metaphorical displacement, but the real, documented kind. The barbershop that got bought out. The family that got pushed to the outer boroughs. My paintings are attempts at restitution, which I know is impossible, which is why I keep painting them.

I work slowly. A painting might take months. I think speed is the enemy of this kind of attention.
