Jean-Michel Basquiat was born in Brooklyn in 1960 to a Haitian father and Puerto Rican mother. At seven, struck by a car, his mother brought him a copy of Grey's Anatomy to recover with — a book that would shape his visual language for life.

He began tagging Lower Manhattan as SAMO© — Same Old Shit — with a friend. By 1982 he was showing internationally. Basquiat worked fast, in Armani suits spattered with paint, across canvas, paper, doors, refrigerators. He died in 1988 at 27.
