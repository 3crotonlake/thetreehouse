Painting & Drawing

The face is never just a face — it's everything that's happened to it.

Donald Sanders paints with the kind of confidence that takes decades to earn. His work moves between two registers — explosive figurative portraits where faces fragment into planes of colour, and fluid sculptural forms that seem to float free of gravity. Whether in oils, mixed media, or bas-relief, there's a persistent tension in his work between control and release, between the recognisable human figure and something more primal beneath it.

There's a Cubist lineage running through Donald's portraits — faces pulled apart and reassembled, eyes that look in two directions at once, features that become abstract geometry without losing their humanity. But this is no academic exercise. The paint is too alive for that, the colour too personal.

His triptych of turquoise figures is something else entirely — bodies in motion, liberated from gravity, rendered with the kind of fluid certainty that suggests years of drawing from life. Warm terracotta grounds against cool teal figures: it's a conversation between earth and air.

— Donald Sanders
