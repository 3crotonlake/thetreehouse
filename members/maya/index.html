Watercolor & Ink
📍 Portland, Oregon

Painting the spaces between memory and place.

I work with watercolor and ink, mostly in small studies that try to hold onto something — a quality of light through a window, the particular green of Pacific Northwest ferns, the way a city looks when you're homesick for it even while you're still there. I paint what I'm afraid I'll forget.

Artist statement

My practice is rooted in attention — looking slowly at things I would otherwise walk past. There's a particular quality of light in the Pacific Northwest, filtered through fir and cedar, that I've been chasing for years. Watercolor suits this because it resists control. You set an intention and then you let the water do what it wants. I think that's honest.

— Maya Chen
